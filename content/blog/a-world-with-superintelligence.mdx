I remember when I first used OpenAI's GPT-3 back in 2021. I felt an immense amount of joy; the fact that I could ask this "thing" whatever came to my mind and its sole purpose was to answer what it was asked.

I remember being incredibly skeptical, and I was right. The model was extremely prone to hallucinations, and so I thought: "maybe these things are just story-telling, and for writing."

Little did I know that over the next few years, these models would become genuinely useful, not just for engineers and lawyers but for anybody wanting to learn, *when used right*.

## Understanding LLMs

Sometimes, I like to take a step back and throw away the math and software prerequisites needed to understand LLMs, it helps me try and see the perspective of the general public.

Imagine opening up an LLM provider's website, and asking any possible question that comes to mind:

> Can you help me file my taxes?
> Is eating 10 apples a day bad?
> ...

As the LLM responds, you're carefully watching the tokens (words) come in, reading as it generates its answer.

Most times, you undoubtedly believe this AI. Well, of course you do, it's been trained on basically the entire internet, you can't even quantify how much more *information* this entity has access to.

The times it's "hallucinating", you're trying to argue with it, persuade it of what you believe to be factually correct, "pushing back".

Without factually verifying an LLM's response, is it that different from what we call fortune tellers?
