A deep multi-layer perceptron built entirely from scratch — no PyTorch `nn.Module`, no autograd. Just raw matrix multiplications, manual backpropagation, and gradient descent. Plus a CNN for comparison.

## Architecture

### MLP (from scratch)

The MLP is a 4-layer fully connected network:

- **Input**: 784 neurons (28×28 pixel grid, flattened)
- **Hidden layers**: 3 layers of 256 neurons each, with ReLU activation
- **Output**: 10 neurons with softmax (one per digit class)

Each hidden layer uses a dual-bias design — a pre-activation bias added before ReLU and a post-activation bias that bypasses the nonlinearity, giving each neuron a linear skip connection past its own activation function.

### CNN

The CNN uses spatial structure that the MLP ignores:

- **Conv1**: 32 filters (3×3) with BatchNorm → ReLU → MaxPool(2×2)
- **Conv2**: 32 filters (3×3) with BatchNorm → ReLU → MaxPool(2×2)
- **FC**: 800 → 10 (flatten and classify)

Convolutional layers detect local patterns (edges, curves) that are translation-invariant — a "7" is recognized whether it's drawn in the center or corner.

## Training

**MLP:**
- Weight initialization: Xavier uniform, scaled per-layer
- Learning rate: 0.5, with cosine annealing decay
- Batch size: 128, 5 epochs
- Loss: Cross-entropy (implemented from scratch)
- The entire training loop — forward pass, backpropagation, and weight updates — is written without any automatic differentiation.

**CNN:**
- Standard PyTorch `nn.Module` with Adam optimizer
- Dropout (0.25) for regularization
- Trained for 50 epochs

## Results

| Model | Test Accuracy | Parameters |
|-------|-------------|------------|
| MLP (from scratch) | 97.76% | ~397K |
| CNN (PyTorch) | 96.58% | ~18K |
